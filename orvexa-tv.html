Frequently asked questions

How do I add a playlist to Orvexa TV?

You can add playlists in two ways: (1) Enter a remote M3U playlist URL in the app settings, or (2) Import a local M3U file from your device storage using the import feature. Both methods are supported on all devices.

What is an M3U file and how do I use it?

An M3U file is a plain text playlist format containing media stream URLs and channel information. You can import M3U files on your device and Orvexa TV will parse the channels for playback. See our M3U playlist guide for detailed instructions.

Does Orvexa TV provide channels or playlists?

No. Orvexa TV is a player application only. It does not provide, host, or distribute any channels or media content. You are responsible for adding your own legal and authorized playlists and content.

Can I use Orvexa TV on Android TV and Fire TV?

Yes. Orvexa TV is fully optimized for Android TV, Google TV, and Fire TV devices with remote control navigation. It also works on Android phones and tablets with touch interface.

Does it support EPG (Electronic Program Guide)?

Yes. Orvexa TV supports EPG data when available in your playlists, providing program information and schedule details for supported channels.

How do I import a local M3U file?

Use the import function in Orvexa TV settings to browse and select M3U files from your device storage. The app will parse the playlist and add channels to your library. See our M3U import guide for step-by-step instructions.

What should I do if playback fails?

First, verify the playlist URL or M3U file is valid and contains working stream links. Check your internet connection, restart the app, and ensure your device has enough storage space. If issues persist, contact support.

Is there a privacy policy for Orvexa TV?

Yes. Please read our privacy policy for complete information on how your data is handled and what permissions the app requires.
