USB OTG Guide

How USB OTG works on Android

USB OTG lets an Android device act as a host for accessories such as flash drives, keyboards, and card readers. Support depends on the device, adapter, storage format, and power needs.

What you need

  • A compatible Android phone, tablet, Android TV box, or Fire TV device.
  • A USB-C or Micro-USB OTG adapter that matches your port.
  • A flash drive formatted in a file system your device can read.
  • A file manager such as Orvexa Files to browse copied files.

Common limitations

  • Some devices cannot power large drives without a powered hub.
  • NTFS support varies across Android builds and TV manufacturers.
  • Apps may need storage permission before they can show external files.
  • Streaming boxes may hide USB options under storage or device preferences.

If the USB drive is not detected

  1. Reconnect the drive and wait a few seconds for Android to mount it.
  2. Try a smaller flash drive or a powered USB hub.
  3. Check whether the drive uses exFAT or FAT32 if your device rejects NTFS.
  4. Restart the Android TV device with the drive connected.
  5. Test the same drive on another Android device to isolate adapter problems.
