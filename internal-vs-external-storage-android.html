Internal storage vs external storage on Android

Android uses storage terms in a confusing way. Internal storage is built into the device. External storage can mean shared media folders, removable USB storage, or an SD card depending on the device.

Storage types explained

Storage areaWhat it containsCan you delete it?
App dataSettings, logins, databases, and saved state.Only if you are ready to reset the app.
CacheTemporary files apps can rebuild.Usually safe, but the app may load slower next time.
DownloadsFiles saved from browsers, transfer tools, and installers.Review first, then delete duplicates and old APKs.
USB or SD cardRemovable media, backups, videos, documents.Yes, but confirm files are backed up.

Best practice

Keep apps and app data on internal storage when possible, and keep large personal media on external storage. Use a file manager such as Orvexa Files to review Downloads and APK folders before clearing space.
